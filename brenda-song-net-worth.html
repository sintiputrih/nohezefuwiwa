What Is Brenda Song’s Net Worth?

Brenda Song, an American actress and producer, has a net worth of $8 million. She began her career as a model during elementary school and is best known for her role as London Tipton on the Disney Channel series “The Suite Life of Zack & Cody” and “The Suite Life on Deck.” She also starred in Hulu’s “Dollface.”

Song has over 80 acting credits to her name, including notable films such as “The Social Network” and “Changeland,” and television series such as “Scandal” and “Station 19.” She has also produced and co-produced several TV movies and episodes of “The Suite Life of Zack & Cody.”

In addition to her on-screen work, Song has lent her voice to various animated projects, including “Lilo & Stitch: The Series” and “The Proud Family: Louder and Prouder.”

Brenda Song Early Life

Brenda Song was born in Carmichael, California in 1988 to a Hmong and Thai family. She began modeling at the age of 5 and appeared in commercials for Little Caesars and Barbie. At the age of 6, Song and her mother moved to Los Angeles for more acting opportunities, and her father and brothers joined them two years later. Song was homeschooled due to her acting career and finished high school at 16 years old. She later attended the University of California, Berkeley, where she majored in psychology and minored in business, graduating in 2009.

Despite wanting to take ballet lessons as a child, Song learned taekwondo alongside her brother. She eventually earned a black belt. As a high school freshman, she was named an All-American Scholar.

Brenda Song’s Relationships and Legal Battle

Brenda Song dated musician Trace Cyrus from 2010 to 2017 and became engaged in 2011. She then started a relationship with co-star Macaulay Culkin in 2017, and they welcomed son Dakota in 2021, named after Culkin’s late sister.

In 2008, Song sued an escort agency for using her photo in an ad without permission. She sought more than $100,000 for “libel, intentional infliction of emotional distress and commercial misappropriation of her photo and likeness.” The defendant was ordered to pay Song $16,000.

Erik Estrada Biography

Erik Estrada is an American actor, voice actor as well as a police officer who was born in East Harlem, New York, United States. Currently, he is known for his lead role in the police drama television series titled CHiPs. Erik is also popular for portraying the role of Francis Llewelyn in a 1977 series that was canceled in 1983.

Erik later became known for his work in Spanish-language telenovelas, his appearances in reality television shows and infomercials as well as regular voice on the Adult Swim series Sealab 2021.

Erik Estrada CHiPs

In CHiPs, Erik plays Francis Llewellyn Poncherello also known as Ponch, a graduate of Lehigh Polytechnical High School where he set the school’s all-time record for visits to the principal’s office. His mother is Maria, and he has two brothers Robert and Martin who live in Chicago, and a sister, Patti who lives in New York.

Before joining the CHP, he worked in a garage as a mechanic. He was convinced by his friend Jon, to join CHP. Ponch was commissioned as an officer of the law in the late summer of 1975. He started off in a patrol car before later becoming a motorcycle officer after completing motor school. After one year and a half into his law enforcement career, Ponch was put on probation and as a result, he was assigned as a partner to Jon Baker.

Ponch can sometimes be seen as a hot head. Estrada is also a keen sportsman. He plays racket-ball and was practically raised on basketball watching the Globetrotters. He was even nicknamed ‘High points Ponch’.

Erik Estrada Accident

During the last season, he was filming a scene where he and his partner jump on the motorcycles, and a car is backing down the alley. The cops chase the car.

It was Friday, and they filmed the scene, and Estrada was in his dressing room when they told him there was a hair in the lens, and they had to film again. But the stunt guys had already gone home.

“They put this young driver in the car,” he said. “The guy stops 25 feet too soon … My motorcycle hits the front fender, I go through the windshield, land on the hood and then the bike flips on my back. Boom, a thousand pounds, crushed.”

A helicopter took Estrada to UCLA, after he fractured ribs, punctured both lungs, cracked his jaw, and broke his wrist. “They gave me five days in intensive care and said I had a 50/50 chance of living,” he said. Estrada got out of the hospital and the world is better for it! “I got back on the bike and here I am rolling now,” he said.

Fat Joe: Career

The Beginnings

Fat Joe began his career in 1992 as a member of the band called Diggin’ in the Crates Crew.

He had the opportunity to work with a variety of musicians, gaining crucial networks, contacts that eventually helped him to develop his record company.

Fat Joe was initially recruited to Relativity Records, and his debut album was scheduled to be released in 1993.

Fat Joe’s debut solo album, Represent, included the song “Flow Joe,” which smashed several records.

In fact, Flow Joe’s track topped the Rap Charts and allowed him to launch his musical journey with a boom.

Ascend to Fame

Fat Joe’s second album, Jealous One’s Envy, was released in 1995, following the success of his previous album.

After that, Fat Joe worked with Nas, Diddy, and Bone Thugs-N-Harmony on his third album, Don Cartegena, which was released in 1998.

Furthermore, the rap musician founded his own record label, Terror Squad. He released his fourth album, Jealous Ones Still Envy (J.O.S.E.), in 2001.

Nonetheless, it was this record that catapulted him to international recognition and prominence.

On top of that, the RIAA labels certified this record as platinum, and the Building Performance Institute, Inc. (BPI) certified it as for silver.

In addition, Fat Joe released his fifth album, True Story, in 2004, which featured one of his most well-known hits, “Lean Back.”

As a result, this track reached the top of the US Billboard Rap song chart and remained on top for three weeks.

Later Stages

Following the success of his fifth album, Fat Joe released his sixth album, All or Nothing, in 2005.

Meanwhile, this album did not achieve the same level of popularity as his previous releases.

Subsequently, a dispute between him and fellow rapper 50 Cent arose due to one of the album’s diss tracks.

The dispute reached a peak in 2005 when Fat Joe insulted 50 Cent at the MTV Video Music Awards show.

As a result, 50 Cent leaped over the stage while screaming profanities.

Eventually, the award presentation was cut and filled with a commercial break.

Despite the circumstances and minor defamation, Fat Joe continued his music career.

Following that, he recorded numerous additional albums, including The elephant in the Room in 2008, Jealous Ones Still Envy 2 (J.O.S.E. 2) in 2009, and The Darkside Vol. 1 in 2010.

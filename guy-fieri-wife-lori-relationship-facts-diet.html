What Guy Fieris Wife Really Thinks About His Greasy Diet

Publish date: 2024-03-01

Guy Fieri's fans wonder if his son Hunter Fieri wants to be like him, as the two have appeared together on some of the Food Network star's shows. Guy Fieri has a high net worth and he has been on so many shows over the years that it's definitely hard to keep track.

Fans are curious about Guy Fieri's diet since his shows aren't usually about eating health food.

What does Guy Fieri eat regularly, and what does his wife Lori think? Let's take a look.

Guy Fieri's Diet

Besides wondering about Guy Fieri's real hair, the food personality is famous for the dishes that he eats on his popular Food Network show since the food is often very rich and indulgent.

As it turns out, Guy Fieri eats healthier than people might assume based on the kind of junk food that he often consumes on Diners, Drive-Ins And Dives. According to Delish.com, he enjoys vegetables and salads.

It sounds like Guy Fieri is super close with his family and he and his wife, Lori, are total foodies who enjoy cooking and eating together. Guy and Lori have two sons, 25-year-old Hunter and 15-year-old Ryder.

Guy talked about his marriage with Today.com and shared that his wife loves when he makes a pasta dish. He said, "My linguini and clams is probably one of her all-time favorites. Especially when the clams are really small. And when we sit down together to have a really nice dinner."

Guy Fieri also shared with Today.com that Lori makes an amazing dish that he loves, and it's sweet to hear that she cooks for him, too. The star said, "There's an old school recipe she makes that she brought home from where she grew up in Rhode Island. It's one of the first things she ever cooked for me and it's still a favorite of mine." Guy shared that its "top sirloin with onions, mushrooms and garlic" with egg noodles and a gravy.

Guy also shared with the publication that his family does have some fun foods but they definitely eat healthy at home: "We indulge from time to time but for the most part, we eat well-balanced, often very plant-based meals."

It sounds like Lori is super supportive of Guy as, according to Mashed.com, Lori wanted Guy to compete on The Next Food Network Star when he was given the chance. Since he was in charge of three restaurants and they were expecting their second baby, he didn't think that it was a good time, but Lori said he had to give it a shot.

Guy And Lori

In an interview with Delish.com, Guy Fieri talked about his family and said that they mean so much to him. He said, "My whole world revolves around my family. That's what sets the tempo for everything I do."

According to Insider.com, Guy and Lori met in 1992 when Guy was in charge of a Long Beach, California restaurant. Lori was there to visit a friend of hers, and Guy could tell that he wanted to be with her. In a romantic story, Guy said that he made Lori a meal and that was that for them. He said, "I don't remember what I made, but I do remember it was quite an epic time. The first time I cooked for her, she was just like, 'You made all this?' We both love food, so being able to cook, that was one of the things that impressed her."

After getting married in 1993, Guy and Lori had their two sons in 1996 and 2005, and they definitely seem to be very happy together.

Lori has joked that she would love for Guy to switch up his hairstyle, as his hair was black when they were first dating, and according to People, his style was different as he would wear suits.

It makes sense that people are super curious about Guy Fieri's diet and it's no wonder that this question comes up a lot when he's interviewed.

The star told Variety that he really isn't into eating fried food and that he's careful with portion sizes. He said, "I’m not a huge fried food fan myself. Personally, I’m a big vegetable fan and I have to be very cautious of what I eat and how much I eat of it. And yes, have they had to come up to me and say ‘Chef, you’ve got two more locations today. You cannot have all the enchiladas’? And have they taken them out of my hands? Yes, they have."

In an interview with People.com, Guy Fieri shared that while he does try all the dishes on Diners, Drive-Ins and Dives, he sticks to a healthy eating and exercise routine.

He said, “I usually exercise before work. Then I drink a big vegetable and fruit juice—we bring a juicer on the road. “I make everybody drink it because I know the immunity, and what the body needs when everybody’s on the road, and working crazy hours.”

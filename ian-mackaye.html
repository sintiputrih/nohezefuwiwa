Biography

Ian MacKaye was born in Washington, DC on April 16, 1962. Famous for fronting the Washington D.C.-based punk bands Fugazi, Minor Threat, The Teen Idles, and Embrace, he is also notable for his work with the indie rock group The Evens. A music producer as well as a punk vocalist, MacKaye produced solo work by Red Hot Chili Peppers guitarist John Frusciante. In his early years, he formed a band called The Slinkees and performed a number titled “I Drink Milk.”

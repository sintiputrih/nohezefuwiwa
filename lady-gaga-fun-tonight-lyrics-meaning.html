Is Lady Gaga's &quot;Fun Tonight&quot; About Christian Carino?

Publish date: 2024-03-11
  • Lady Gaga has released her sixth studio album, Chromatica, which the singer said was inspired by pain and heartbreak from previous relationships.
  • Fans believe that one of the album's tracks, "Fun Tonight," is about Gaga's ex-fiancé Christian Carino.

Lady Gaga has already opened up about how her newest album, Chromatica, was influenced by pain and heartbreak from relationships past. The superstar's fiercest fans, however, are convinced that one of the album's tracks specifically shades her ex-fiancé Christian Carino.

The song in question, titled "Fun Tonight," details Gaga realizing the dissolution of a relationship, and how her mental health and experiences with fame also affect her love life.

"You love the paparazzi, love the fame / Even though you know it causes me pain / I feel like I'm in a prison hell / Stick my hands through the steel bars and yell," sings Gaga on the revealing record.

Twitter was quick to point out how the lyrics seem to echo the speculation surrounding Gaga and Carino's breakup last year. Though the singer notoriously remains mum regarding her relationships, a source told Us Weekly that Carino "didn't treat Gaga very well toward the end of their [two-year] relationship," while another claimed Carino was "jealous" of Gaga following the immense success of her film A Star Is Born.

Gaga clarified the true meaning of the track during an interview with Beats 1 host Zane Lowe, however, explaining that the song details her ongoing struggles with mental health while navigating her career.

"In the middle of the record, 'Fun Tonight,' which is a song that means a lot to me, and every time I listened to it, I get choked up because I can't tell you how many nights that people that really love me were trying to get me to smile or laugh or be optimistic, and I just had no ability to be happy. It just wasn't there," said Gaga.

The Jimbo Fisher Era

The Texas A&M tenure under Jimbo Fisher was characterized by a notable lack of success. Despite a substantial financial commitment with a guaranteed $117,450,000 contract, the Aggies did not secure any national, conference, or division titles during Fisher’s tenure. The team also failed to achieve a single 10-win season and encountered difficulties in winning against ranked opponents on away games. Remarkably, Fisher’s performance record proved to be inferior to that of his predecessor, Kevin Sumlin.

Financial Impact

Ending Jimbo Fisher’s contract will be a substantial financial undertaking for Texas A&M, given the staggering buyout amount of $76 million. This significant financial commitment is poised to have a notable impact on the school’s budget as they embark on the search for a new head coach. Beyond the buyout, additional expenses will accrue from assistant coach buyouts and the recruitment of new staff, potentially amounting to a total expenditure exceeding $150 million.

Reasons for Termination

Speculation surrounding Fisher’s termination hints at its potential correlation with the impending entry of the Texas Longhorns into the SEC. Given the intense rivalry between Texas A&M and Texas, there may be a strategic desire to avoid trailing behind the Longhorns, particularly as they prepare to compete in the same conference. Fisher’s perceived lack of connections to Texas and his inability to assimilate into the program have contributed to the perception that his 10-year, $95 million contract stands as one of the ill-fated decisions in the history of college football coaching.

Potential Candidates

Among the possible candidates, Jeff Traylor and Mike Elko emerge as the co-favorites to succeed Fisher. Traylor, credited with guiding UTSA to consecutive Conference USA titles and earning the league’s coach of the year distinction, stands out as a compelling option. His relatively modest $7 million buyout adds to his appeal. On the other hand, Elko, formerly a defensive coordinator under Fisher at Texas A&M and presently the head coach of the Duke Blue Devils, comes with a $5 million buyout. Despite facing challenges, including injuries, during his tenure at Duke, Elko remains a noteworthy contender for the position.

Other Contenders

Kalen DeBoer, the incumbent coach of an undefeated Washington team with aspirations for a Pac-12 title and a berth in the College Football Playoff, emerges as another viable candidate. Similar to Fisher, DeBoer places a strong emphasis on offensive strategies, boasting an impressive 21-2 record over the course of two seasons. Meanwhile, Dan Lanning from Oregon, with a substantial $20 million buyout, is also in contention for the position, bringing his coaching expertise to the forefront of considerations.

Notable Options

Lane Kiffin, currently at the helm of Ole Miss, commands a salary of approximately $9 million and brings significant experience and visibility to the coaching landscape. Additional prospective candidates feature Mike Norvell from Florida State, James Franklin from Penn State, and notable figures such as Urban Meyer and Deion Sanders. However, Meyer seems content with his television career at the present moment, and Sanders is unlikely to depart from Colorado while his sons are actively playing there.

Current Odds

In the ongoing quest for the next head coach at Texas A&M, oddsmakers have established betting odds for potential candidates. The current favorites include Jeff Traylor and Mike Elko, with Kalen DeBoer, Dan Lanning, Eli Drinkwitz, Lance Leipold, Chris Klieman, PJ Fleck, Kliff Kingsbury, James Franklin, Lane Kiffin, Mike Norvell, Urban Meyer, and Deion Sanders rounding out the list of contenders.

In summary, Texas A&M’s move to part ways with Jimbo Fisher has ignited speculation about the future head coach for the Aggies’ football program. A roster of potential candidates, coupled with odds provided by oddsmakers, signifies the commencement of the search for Fisher’s successor. The unfolding events will reveal who will step into the role and guide the Aggies toward future success.

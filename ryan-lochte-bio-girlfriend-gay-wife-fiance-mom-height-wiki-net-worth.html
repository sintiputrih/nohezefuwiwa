Ryan Lochte is also dazzled by Olympic medals like pearls in a deep sea oyster. The twelve-time Olympic swimmer has won six gold, three silver and three bronze medals. He is certainly an athlete to be envied by most and admired by others. Ryan is arguably the best Olympic swimmer after Michael Phelps.

His praise is not limited to the Olympics, however. Over the course of his career and spanning all the competitions he has entered, Ryan has collected 90 medals. 54 gold, 22 silver and 14 bronze. Lochte won the 2013 Swammy Award for US Swimmer of the Year. He also picked up the World Swimmer of the Year and USA Swimmer of the Year twice, and the FINA Female Swimmer of the Year three times.

For his efforts with Team USA, Lochted holds the world record for the 4×200 meter freestyle relay (long course) and the 4×100 meter freestyle relay (mixed). Solo, he holds the world record for the 200m individual medley (long and short course) and the 400m individual medley (short course).

Youth and career

Ryan is half Dutch and half Cuban thanks to his parents. He was born in Rochester, New York and raised in Bristol where he attended Bloomfield Central Schools. Ryan’s parents taught him to swim when he was five years old. Her father got a job helping coach swimming when she was 12 years old. This job meant that the family had to move to Florida.

He didn’t take swimming seriously and always hurt other kids in the water. However, his attitude towards swimming changed at age 14, when he lost at the Junior Olympics.

In 2007, Ryan Lochte graduated from the University of Florida with a major in sports management. He swam for the Florida Swim and Dive Team in the National Collegiate Athletic Association (NCAA). The Olympian also swam in the Southeastern Conference competition for four years, from 2004 to 2007. One of the most vivid memories from this period of his career is when he broke the NCAA record nearly ten years established by Tom Dolan in the 400m individual medley.

Ryan’s Olympic debut came in 2008. This edition of the Olympics saw Ryan Lochte win a team gold medal and an individual gold medal and a team and team bronze medal. The professional swimmer continued to collect medals and accolades in the years leading up to the 2012 Olympics in London. He rounded off his efforts in London with 5 medals: two gold. two silver and one bronze. One gold and one silver were individual honours.

At the Rio Olympics in 2016, Ryan won another gold medal, bringing his Olympic medal tally to twelve.

Ryan’s efforts don’t end in a pool. He appeared on the episodic shows 30 Rocks and 90210, both in 2012. He also participated with professional dancer Cheryl Burke in the dance show, Dancing with the Stars. In 2013, he launched his own reality show called What Would Ryan Lochte Do? It aired one season of eight episodes before being canceled five weeks later.

The decorated Olympian has had his share of controversies too. At the Rio Olympics in August 2016, he and teammate Jimmy Feigen claimed they were robbed along with two of their teammates. A police report and surveillance video later dispelled their charges and threatened them with 18 months in prison under Brazilian law. The swimmer ends up serving a 10-month swimming ban. A suspension he says made him stronger.

The family of Ryan Lochte, mom

Ryan’s father, Steven Lochte, is of German, Dutch and English heritage. His mother, Ileana Lochte (formerly Aramburu) was born and raised in Havana, Cuba. He has four siblings; Kristin, Megan, Devon and Brandon.

Ryan obviously shares a strong bond with his mother. She showed her penchant for always coming to her son’s defense during an interview on Good Morning America. Illeana defended her son, Ryan’s bleached hair, at the 2016 Olympics in Rio. She said he was the kind of person who liked to do whatever he wanted and she got used to it. Although she admitted she wasn’t a fan of the hair color, she said Ryan looked cute in it. She also said it was much better than the American flag-themed grid he noticed at the previous Olympics.

Illeana also helped ignite the fire surrounding the news that her son and his teammates were robbed at the Rio Olympics. She told Fox Sports in an interview that the incident happened at gunpoint.

Ryan Lochte’s Relationships – Girlfriend, Gay, Wife, Fiance

Ryan is definitely not gay and may be married now, but he has dated a few other celebrities in the past. In 2011, he was in a relationship with American singer K. Michelle. They ended after about a year of being together.

Subsequently, rumors spread that he was dating Australian swimmer Blair Evans. Her mother dispelled the rumor during her 2012 interview with US Weekly. According to her, Ryan was too focused on his career to think about relationships.

During the Rio 2016 Olympics, it became public knowledge that Ryan Lochte was dating Kayla Rae Reid. Kayla is a former Playboy roommate. She was the Playboy Playmate of the Month for July 2015. Net Worth – How rich is Ryan Lochte?

Ryan did well. The veteran Olympian has enough endorsement deals and trade appearances to net him a sizable fortune. He has signed endorsement deals with Speedo, Gillette, Gatorade, Nissan, AT&T, Ralph Lauren, Procter and Gamble and many more. He has also appeared in advertisements promoting the Nissan Altima and has appeared on the covers of Vogue, Time, Men’s Journal and Men’s Health.

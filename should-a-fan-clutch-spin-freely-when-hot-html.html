Should a fan clutch spin freely when hot?

Publish date: 2024-07-08

Answer

Sticking a screwdriver in the fan while it’s hot is a surefire way to tell if it’s shot. If the fan comes to a halt, the clutch is faulty. If the screwdriver is thrown back at you by the fan, the clutch is in good working order. When the vehicle is turned off, the fan should be able to be moved freely by hand.

In a similar vein, you might wonder if a clutch fan is supposed to be able to spin freely.

The fan should move freely by hand when the vehicle is off. Any significant resistance indicates that it has locked up.

Similarly, what temperature does a fan clutch engage?

Answer: The majority of fan clutches engage at temperatures of approximately 170°F air (approximately 180-190°F engine). They reduce the temperature about 20°F before disengaging.

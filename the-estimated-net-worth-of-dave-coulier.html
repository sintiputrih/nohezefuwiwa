Biography of Dave Coulier

David Coulier, known for his portrayal of Joey Gladstone in the popular sitcom “Full House,” is a Canadian-German-Jewish descent comedian, impressionist, actor, and voice actor, born on 21 September 1959 in St. Claire Shores, Michigan, USA. Coulier began his acting career in 1979 and has since amassed an estimated net worth of $4 million, primarily from his work on various TV shows and films.

Net Worth: $4 Million

After leaving Notre Dame High School in Michigan in 1977, Coulier pursued his passion for comedy, which ultimately contributed to his accumulating net worth. He ventured into voice acting in 1979 with the comedy horror TV series “Scooby and Scrappy-Doo” and continued to lend his voice to several productions, including “The Jetsons,” “Muppet Babies,” “Robot Chicken,” and “Farce of the Penguins.” Coulier’s hosting roles on shows like “America’s Funniest People” and “America’s Most Talented Kid” also added to his financial success.

Coulier’s extensive filmography, which includes roles in “The Thirteenth Year,” “The Even Stevens Movie,” and appearances on TV shows like “Lois & Clark: The New Adventures of Superman,” further solidified his net worth. Known for his clean comedy routines and his impersonations of celebrities and cartoon characters, Coulier’s diverse talents have significantly contributed to his financial prosperity.

On a personal note, Coulier married producer and photographer Melissa Bring in 2014, after dating her since 2005. His hobbies extend to flying as he owns and pilots a Beechcraft B35.

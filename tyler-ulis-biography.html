Who is Tyler Ulis?

Tyler Ulis is an American professional basketball player who is currently for the Phoenix Suns of the National Basketball Association (NBA).

He played college basketball for the Kentucky Wildcats. He helped the Kentucky Wildcats reach 2015 Final Four as a freshman.

Tyler Ulis: Birth Facts, Family, Childhood

Tyler was born on January 5, 1996, in Southfield, Michigan, United States. He is an American by nationality and is of African-American ethnicity.

He is the son of African-American parents. His father’s name is James Ulis. He grew up in Lima, Ohio with his mother, Kelly Reed.

Tyler Ulis has four brothers namely James Ulis, Jaden Reed, Ahron Ulis, and Anton Porter Ulis. Ulis ran track as a youth and competed in the 800 meters and 1500 meters.

In which school did Tyler Ulis attend?

For high school education, he moved to Matteson, Illinois, and lived with his father.

Tyler Ulis enrolled at the Marian Catholic School and started his first game as a freshman against future Illinois Fighting Illini men’s basketball.

Tyler Ulis: Professional Life, Career

Tyler Ulis began his basketball career playing for Marian Catholic School in Matteson, Illinois.

He was also named to the 36-man 2014–15 Bob Cousy Award Preseason Watch List and the Wayman Tisdale Award Watch List.

Tyler Ulis played college basketball for the Kentucky Wildcats and helped the club reach 2015 Final Four as a freshman.

Tyler Ulis was selected for the 2014 McDonald’s All-American Game on January 29. He began his professional career in the year 2016.

Tyler was signed by the Phoenix Suns for the 2016 NBA draft. He made his debut with the Suns on October 26, 2016, against the Sacramento Kings.

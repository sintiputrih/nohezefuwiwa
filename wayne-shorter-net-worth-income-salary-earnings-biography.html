Wayne Shorter Net Worth, Income, Salary, Earnings, Biography

Publish date: 2024-09-14

Wayne Shorter (born August 25, 1933) is a jazz saxophonist and composer from the United States.

Shorter is “probably jazz’s greatest living small-group composer and a contender for greatest living improviser,” according to New York Times jazz critic Ben Ratliff. Shorter’s compositions have become jazz standards in many cases. His work has received worldwide acclaim, critical acclaim, and numerous accolades, including ten Grammy Awards. He’s also been praised for his mastery of the soprano saxophone (after switching from the tenor in the late 1960s), starting a long reign as Down Beat’s annual poll-winner on that instrument in 1970, winning the critics’ vote for ten years and the readers’ poll for eighteen. Shorter rose to fame in the late 1950s as a member of Art Blakey’s Jazz Messengers, ultimately becoming their main composer. He later joined Miles Davis’ Second Great Quintet in the 1960s, and then co-founded the jazz fusion band Weather Report. As a bandleader, he has released over 20 CDs.

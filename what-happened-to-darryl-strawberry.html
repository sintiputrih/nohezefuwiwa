What happened to Darryl Strawberry?

Publish date: 2024-09-16

Darryl Strawberry, born on March 12, 1962, in Los Angeles, faced a tumultuous childhood marked by his father's abusive behavior.

Despite this, he found solace in sports, particularly baseball, and his talent ultimately led to his selection by the New York Mets in the 1980 Major League Baseball draft.

Strawberry quickly rose through the ranks, making his major league debut in 1983 and earning the National League Rookie of The Year award that season.

Prime Years and Personal Struggles

From 1983 to 1990, Strawberry played for the Mets and became a prominent figure in the National League.

While achieving considerable success on the field, he also faced challenges off the field, including disruptive behavior and personal struggles with substance abuse, particularly cocaine

. Despite these challenges, Strawberry's talent shone through, and he became known for his powerful hitting and athletic abilities.

Later Career and Health Issues

After his time with the Mets, Strawberry's career took him to other teams, including the Los Angeles Dodgers, San Francisco Giants, and the New York Yankees.

But his playing career was marred by legal and health problems, including a battle with colon cancer and repeated issues with drug addiction and run-ins with the law.

Personal Redemption and Charity Work

Despite his tumultuous past, Darryl Strawberry found redemption through his faith. He became an evangelical born-again Christian, appearing on various platforms to share his testimony and newfound Christian faith.

He also dedicated himself to charity work, particularly for children affected by autism. In 2006, he married Tracy, and together they founded "The Darryl Strawberry Foundation" to support children with autism.

His son, Darryl "D.J." Strawberry, became a successful basketball player, and Strawberry's family life became a central focus for him.

Retirement and Health Struggles

Strawberry's retirement from baseball did not signal an end to his personal struggles.

He continued to face legal and health challenges, including multiple arrests, probation violations, and health issues such as a heart attack in 2024.

Despite these difficulties, he remained committed to his faith and charity work, using his experiences to help others facing similar challenges.

Legacy and Recognition

Despite the hardships he faced, Darryl Strawberry's impact on baseball and his personal journey have not gone unnoticed.

He has been honored by both the New York Mets and the New York Yankees, inducted into the Mets Hall of Fame, and had his number 18 retired by the Mets in a ceremony during the 2024 baseball season.

Darryl Strawberry's life has been a testament to the highs and lows that can accompany athletic success.

Despite personal struggles and setbacks, his commitment to his faith and charity work reflects a journey of redemption and hope.

His legacy in baseball and his dedication to helping others serve as a powerful example of resilience and perseverance in the face of adversity.

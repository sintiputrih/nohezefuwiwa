Where will the 2024 Olympics be held?

Publish date: 2024-04-07

The 2024 Summer Olympics, also known as the Games of the XXXIII Olympiad, are scheduled to be held in Paris, France.

Paris: The Host City

Paris, the capital city of France, has been chosen as the host city for the 2024 Summer Olympics. This decision was made by the International Olympic Committee (IOC) during its 131st Session in Lima, Peru, on September 13, 2017. The city of Paris was awarded the Olympic Games, and it will be the first time since 1924 that Paris will host the Summer Olympics, making it a historic event for the city.

Olympic Events and Venues

The 2024 Olympic Games in Paris are expected to feature a wide array of sporting events, showcasing the talents of athletes from around the world. The aim is to have two routes available for sports fans during the 2024 Olympic Games: Paris-Issy-les-Moulineaux heliport to Saint-Cyr airfield, and Paris-Charles de Gaulle and Le Bourget airports and Paris city, on a site to be determined.

Innovative Transportation for the Olympics

In an effort to create a better future and embrace sustainable mobility, the 2024 Olympics in Paris are exploring innovative transportation options. One such initiative involves the use of electric aircraft and emission-free flights, with Volocopter being at the forefront of this pioneering spirit. Volocopter, a company that unveiled its VoloCity concept in 2015, has developed numerous prototypes of the aircraft and carried out 1,500 test flights. The company aims to make urban flights just a few smartphone clicks away, marking a significant advancement in global mobility.

Cultural Significance

The Olympics are not only about sports but also about celebrating culture and national pride. The 2006 Winter Olympics in Torino, Italy, is a testament to this, as it featured a triumphant display of national pride, including costumes by renowned designers such as Giorgio Armani and Moschino, as well as music by the legendary Luciano Pavarotti. This memorable event showcased Italy's rich cultural heritage and left a lasting impression on the audience, especially with Pavarotti's final public performance of the aria “Nessun Dorma” from Puccini's Turandot.
